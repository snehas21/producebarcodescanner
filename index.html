Tap "Start camera" and point it at a striped barcode. Fruit stickers are often creased/curved and hard to scan - typing the PLU number below is usually faster.

Or enter a code / name

Loose-produce stickers (banana, apple, etc.) show a 4-5 digit PLU number - some have a real barcode too, but it's often creased or curved around the fruit and won't scan reliably even with a good camera, so typing the number here is usually faster. Packaged items use a longer 8-14 digit barcode the camera can scan normally.

PLU lookups use a small hand-checked reference list, not the full official IFPS database - always confirm anything that matters against store signage. Barcode (UPC/EAN) lookups query the free, crowd-sourced Open Food Facts database and may not cover every Canadian product.

In Canada, produce sold as "organic" must be certified under the Canada Organic Regime (CFIA) - look for the Canada Organic logo.
